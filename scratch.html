The following is a step by step procedure of how the mechanics of a general golf swing should be done. The directions are for right handed golfers. If you are left hand, apply the opposites.

  1. The Setup
    • Feet should be spread out a little more than shoulder width apart
    • When gripping the club, interlock your pinky finger of your right hand with your index finger of your left hand
    • Make sure your feet are pointed to your target, feel free to open your toes for comfort
    • Shoulders and hips should be square and parallel with when your feet are pointing
  2. The Backswing
    • Start backswing off slow and steaady
    • Keep your left arm (elbow) straight throughout the entire backswing
    • The chicken wing of your right arm should be as connected as possible to your body, try not to separate them too much
  3. The Downswing
    • Once you hit the top, the downswing should progressively get faster, don't be too fast at the top
    • Hips and hands should be in line together as you turn back to the ball and head into the follow through
  4. The Follow Through
    • Finish strong and through the ball
    • Hold your pose at the top as you watch the ball
    • Keep in mind the famouse quote: "In golf as in life, it is the follow through that makes the difference."
